# How to Get a Firmware Engineering Job — Roadmap

## Step 1: Choose Your Degree Path

### Computer Science
- Totally viable for firmware — you'll need extra curriculars to cover the hardware gap
- Take a **computer engineering or embedded software minor**
- Supplement with embedded systems projects and FPGA/RTL research

### Electrical / Computer Engineering
- Strong hardware foundation; add a **CS minor** for data structures, algorithms, and software depth
- CS classes synergize extremely well with EE — puts the "why" behind the hardware

## Step 2: Build Curriculars (The Real Differentiators)

### Embedded Systems Projects
- Solve **real, personal problems** with hardware (microcontroller + sensors/actuators)
- Don't pick generic tutorials — unique projects stand out and are easier to talk about passionately
- Must involve physical hardware: register mapping, peripherals, real pins

### Research
- FPGA/RTL chip design, TinyML/edge ML, compiler research
- Builds relationships with professors (→ recommendation letters)
- Publications on your resume are a massive differentiator
- **TinyML** is a huge trend: compressing ML models for edge hardware (security cameras, drones, robots)
- **Compiler research:** new AI accelerators (TPUs, NPUs) all need custom software stacks

### Internships
- Do embedded/firmware internships — leverage projects and research to land them
- Not optional in today's competitive market

### Clubs
- Formula SAE, robotics clubs — real team projects with hardware
- More useful for undergrads; masters students should prioritize research

## Step 3: Portfolio & Resume
- **Portfolio website:** photos, videos, hardware close-ups of your projects
  - Not a file list — show the problem, solution, and technical details
  - Link it from your resume
  - Don't put work experience on portfolio (that's what the resume is for)
- **Resume:** education → experience → research → projects → skills
- **STAR method:** for every project, know the Situation, Task, Action, Result
- Practice explaining every resume item out loud

## Step 4: Interview Prep
- Study firmware-specific interview problems (bit manipulation, state machines, ring buffers, etc.)
- Also prepare for DSA questions — C++, Python, DFS/BFS, hashmaps
- Rehearse project explanations — sound passionate and knowledgeable

## Step 5: Apply
- The market is competitive but opportunity is massive
- Amazon (satellites), SpaceX, AI chip companies all hiring heavily
- Firmware pays on par with high-level SWE — less competition, same compensation

## On Master's Degrees
- **Not required** if you put in work during undergrad
- Useful as a "reset" — more time for projects, research, portfolio, internships
- The diploma alone doesn't get you hired — the curriculars do
